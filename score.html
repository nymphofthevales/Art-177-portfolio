Sounds and Signs

The Score

Project: Symbols created to represent three sounds.

Notes: For this project, I took a more abstract approach. This was motivated by three questions:

  • I'm interested in the simple objects around us, and what past they hold, what stories they can convey—what is the "soul" of an object?
  • The original project prompt asked for three sounds made by the mouth, but this begs the question: what about sounds not made by the mouth? What about sounds that can scarcely exist in reality, the sounds created in dreams and visions and imagination? How could these sounds be evoked, if not by the mouth?
  • What constitutes a symbol? A symbol can be a drawn figure, like a letter or shape, but in the science of semiotics—the study of symbols and signs—a symbol can be anything that conveys meaning to the interpreter.

Thus in total my motivation became: how can seemingly mundane objects act as symbols, vessels for meaning, to convey otherwise unimaginable sounds or soundscapes? The result was these three illustrations.
