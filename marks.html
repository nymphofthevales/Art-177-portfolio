Unconscious Marks

February 7

Subject: Whatever "unconscious marks" could be found around the room; things created unintentionally, borne out of chaos, nonetheless leaving some mark on the architectural space which we generally consider intentional in design.

Notes: The subjects I mostly focused on were what seemed to be hard water residue (calcium/lime/mineral buildup) on the concrete of the wall, then later on air bubbles in the concrete itself. In their random patterns, I found myself discovering all sorts of clouds, islands, maps, orbiting diagrams... In fact, the cloud analogy is most fitting, since the act of looking at clouds is similar in purpose.

...the patterns so delicate that perhaps their subtle tracery can escape the termites' gnawing, corruption's gangrene which in time spreads itself across the empire. Marco Polo begins to tell the Khan of another city, Berenice, which grows in concentric rings... [Paraphrased from Italo Calvino's Invisible Cities]

...and the islands arrange themselves into a million forms, all arrayed across the sky, displaying cornices, columns, arches and alleyways. When the Khan looks over them he sees the borders of his empire, spread out like the lines of a hand, reaching, reaching...
