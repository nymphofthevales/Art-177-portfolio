Deconstruction

Take Apart

Project: Illustrate the internals of some device in order to better understand it.

Notes: This strange device is one which I found in a military antique store downtown. After researching the maker's marks on the side, I found that it's supposedly a ventilation valve for an old airplane, possibly form WWII. The orbiting center part opens up to allow pressurized air to flow through. Similar air valves, though usually smaller, may be recognized in modern commercial airplanes or cross-country busses, set into the ceiling alongside controls for, say, a booklight in order to allow the passenger control over air flow.

I created a few sketches of the whole object, then set out making an animation of its disassembly. If the video does not load, it can be watched on Youtube.
